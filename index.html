DineIn: Restuarant Management

This project was for an undergraduate class that focused on obtaining experience in a work-like environment. I was placed in a group of six students and we were given absolute freedom to develop an application we thought to be challenging to us and helpful to a community.

DineIn is an application created to faciliate managing a restaurant. We followed the Agile Development Process by having two meetings a week, one for discussion and the other for code reviews. We split the development into three teams: UI/UX, Back-end, and Functionality. I was part of the UI/UX team and I contributed by creating the logo and creating the minimalist overall theme. As part of this team, I also ran several user tests to ensure the application was user-friendly. I prepared experience questionnaires, user interviews, and reviewed the results with the team. Below are several screenshots of the latest iteration. Unfortunately, the project was not fully finished due to the team disbanding.

Lambda Theta Phi: Gamma Theta Chapter

Throughout my undergraduate career, I worked as a Graphic Designer/Web Developer for Lambda Theta Phi Latin Fraternity Inc, an active organization on the UW Campus. The first design shown was for the branding of the established chapter at the University of Wisconsin-Madison and it is currently being used on all media platforms. The second design was a banner for their Facebook page. The shirts were designed for members of the organization from all over the nation. The last two were fliers that were designed for some of their events. I followed their National Branding Guide to ensure all designs were not violating their guidelines.
